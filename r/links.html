The Noam Chomsky Website

Noam Chomsky has probably been the most influential single individual in my political make up. His influence isn't only confined to me. He single handedly revolutionised the field of linguistics, provided major contributions the fields of computer science and psychology, was a very early outspoken critic of the Vietnam war, was involved in helping release the Watergate Papers, and has published over 50 books, given countless lectures and is apparently the most quoted American in the English language.

It has been said to not be aware of Chomsky is to court genuine ignorance, and certainly for me it was true, it wasn't until I read Chomsky that I pulled together a cohesive world view. I'd encourage you to enlist the help of Chomsky in forming (or challenging) your own world view. His political and historical analysis is always sharp, but also try and find something he says about anarchism as well. It was through Chomsky that I was exposed to an intelligent form of anarchism, and came to embrace it almost because I agreed so closely with his principles that "whatever he is, I must be".

On this site you can choose from video, audio and text. I'd also encourage you to go to video.google.co.uk and look up Manufacturing Consent, a documentary made about him.

Z Magazine

I even met the guy who started ZNet once after a talk Michael Albert. He was giving a talk on his concept for a fair economic scheme, Parecon, or participatory economics. The most interesting part of it was what he called balanced job complexes. He saw some work as empowering and some as disempowering, and that this had a number of ramifications, noticeably in the political realm. His idea was to for people to share jobs so that everyone got an equal measure of the two. Anyway a lot more to say about that, but he is just one of the many alternative writers featured regularly on this site. I subscribed for many years to his online articles, until they failed to fix a security flaw in their online subscriber system I told them about, but during that time I read many wonderful daily articles from authors which included Arundhati Roy, Noam Chomsky, George Monbiot, William Blum, Naomi Klein, Vandana Shiva and many more. The site is an absolute wealth of information, it's one of the best out there for finding out what's going on in the world. The site has been redesigned heavily since the old days, so maybe they fixed up their security :)

Democracy Now

I don't have a television, the main source of news for me is reading and watching the daily broadcast of Democracy Now. Hosted by Amy Goodman, who was one of the reporters who survived the Dili Massacre in East Timor, and Juan Gonzalez, a long time activist who was in the Puerto Rican version of the black panthers, the young lords, Democracy Now considers itself "the exception to the rulers". Independent or alternative media like this is so important in getting a balanced overall view. Of course it has been so successful it has some wonderful guests on it, from Mary Robinson and Noam Chomsky, to the heads of developing nations, to young people in the streets protesting, you get the voices that are so often silent in the mainstream media. Available as a pod cast, downloadable video, transcript and embedded video, plenty of ways to keep up with it.
